CET Exam 2024: Everything You Need to Know

Overview

The Common Entrance Test (CET) is a state-level entrance exam conducted for admissions to various undergraduate programs like Engineering, Pharmacy, Agriculture, and Veterinary courses. Different states conduct their own versions of the CET exam, including KCET, MHT-CET, and others, for admissions into government and private colleges within the respective states.

What is the CET Exam?

The CET exam is a common entrance test conducted by various states to screen candidates for admission into undergraduate courses such as Engineering, Pharmacy, and Agriculture. The exam is usually conducted after the 12th grade. CET is based on subjects like Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, and Biology, and the format may vary depending on the course and state.

CET 2024: Latest Updates

CET 2024 introduces a few updates regarding the exam format and application process. Some states have introduced an online mode of examination. The syllabus remains consistent with previous years, focusing on 12th-grade topics in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics (or Biology, depending on the stream).

Eligibility Criteria

  • Candidates must have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) from a recognized board.
  • Minimum aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology as per the state guidelines (generally around 50-60%).
  • No age limit for CET in most states.

CET Registration 2024: Step-by-Step Process

Follow the steps below to register for CET 2024:

  1. Visit the official website of the respective state's CET board.
  2. Complete the online registration by entering your personal and academic details.
  3. Upload the required documents like photograph, signature, and ID proof.
  4. Pay the application fee (varies by state).
  5. Submit the application form and keep a printout for future reference.

Preparation Tips

  • Understand the exam pattern and syllabus thoroughly.
  • Focus on strengthening your basics in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics/Biology.
  • Practice previous years' CET papers to understand question trends.
  • Attempt mock tests to improve time management and accuracy.
  • Focus on revising key concepts regularly.

Result and Cut-Off

The CET 2024 results will be declared approximately 4-6 weeks after the exam. The result can be checked on the official state CET website. The cut-off scores for different courses vary depending on the exam's difficulty level and the number of seats available in colleges.

How to Send CET Scores to Colleges

CET scores are automatically considered for admission to state colleges. Candidates can specify their course preferences during the counselling process based on their CET score. Admission is based on merit and availability of seats in the desired colleges.
